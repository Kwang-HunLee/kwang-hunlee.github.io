About

Hi, I'm Kwang-Hun Lee (이광훈) — an environmental engineer based in Pohang, South Korea. I am completing my Ph.D. in Water Resources Engineering at POSTECH under Prof. Jonghun Kam, and will continue as a postdoctoral researcher at the POSTECH Environmental Research Institute starting later this year.

My work sits at the intersection of field hydrology, big-data analytics, and remote-sensing AI. I drive uncrewed surface vehicles across Korean agricultural reservoirs, fly multispectral drones above them, and reconcile that high-resolution truth with Sentinel-2 satellite observations through machine-learning models that are designed to be explainable, not just accurate.

At the macro scale, I use rotated PCA, hybrid ensemble ML, and XAI on multi-decadal records of 3,300+ agricultural reservoirs to extract dominant spatiotemporal patterns of water quantity, quality, and agricultural water use. At the local scale, I run a 7-year high-resolution USV deployment on a single reservoir as ground truth. The goal is to bridge the resolution-coverage gap — turning scattered sensors into a coherent picture of water and nutrient cycling from a single waterbody to the whole country, and ultimately, the globe.

Research

A thesis built on four chapters, bridging the resolution–coverage gap.

My Ph.D. thesis — Monitoring of Water and Nutrient Cycles in Anthropocene from Local to Global Scales — asks how advanced monitoring technologies can be combined to characterise the coupled dynamics of water quantity and quality across spatial scales, from individual reservoirs to global networks. Four chapters tackle that question from complementary angles.

Two dominant modes of daily water storage volume across 3,300+ Korean agricultural reservoirs, separating central from southern regional regimes.

Chapter 1 · Macro scale

Big-data analytics on a national reservoir network

I apply rotated PCA and time-series decomposition to multi-decadal records of ~3,300 agricultural reservoirs across South Korea, extracting the dominant spatiotemporal modes of water storage, total organic carbon, and agricultural water use. The same framework exposes how adaptive rice-transplanting schedules are shifting under a warming climate — revealing emerging hydroclimatic vulnerability invisible in any single-station view.

  • Rotated PCA
  • Time-series clustering
  • National-scale hydrology
  • Climate adaptation

Lee & Kam, Water Research (2024) · Lee, Nam & Kam, Agricultural Water Management (in review)

Catamaran USV instrumented with YSI EXO2 multi-parameter sonde, rQPOD nitrate analyser, and ADCP for high-resolution dual monitoring of water quantity & quality.

Chapter 2 · Local scale

USV high-resolution reservoir monitoring

Over five years and 100+ surveys at Daljeon reservoir, I built a high-resolution dual-monitoring record — water depth, temperature, DO, pH, EC, and nitrate — that resolves within-reservoir spatial heterogeneity conventional point sampling cannot see. Conventional nitrate-load estimates can overshoot by +30.8 % ± 5.1 % when this heterogeneity is ignored.

  • USV
  • YSI EXO2 · rQPOD · ADCP
  • Bathymetry
  • Nitrate load

Lee et al., Water Resources Research (2023) · Lee et al., ACS ES&T Water (in review)

Three platforms — in-situ USV, UAV multispectral imagery, and Sentinel-2 — integrated for six-year reconstruction of reservoir turbidity and fDOM.

Chapter 3 · Multi-platform fusion

USV + UAV + Sentinel-2 with explainable AI

To carry local truth to satellite coverage, I bias-correct Sentinel-2 reflectance against UAV multispectral orthomosaics using a hybrid Multi-Ensemble Machine Learning (hMEML) retrieval, then predict turbidity and fDOM with tree ensembles, deep nets, and CNNs. Permutation-importance XAI exposes which Sentinel-2 bands, spectral indices, and meteorological drivers actually carry the signal — so the model is interpretable, not just accurate.

  • Sentinel-2
  • UAV multispectral
  • hMEML · RF · DNN · CNN
  • XAI / permutation importance

Lee & Kam, Sentinel-2 reservoir fusion (in prep.)

From point measurement, to 10 m grid, to multi-platform integration: a roadmap toward a global inland-water observatory.

Chapter 4 · Synthesis

Toward a global inland-water observatory

The thesis closes with a discussion of how the three preceding chapters chain together — local USV truth refines UAV inversions; UAV bias-corrects Sentinel-2; satellites carry the result to thousands of reservoirs; the nationwide rPCA framework places each reservoir in regional context. The end goal: a transferable framework for next-generation global water-resources management.

  • Cross-scale integration
  • SWOT & Sentinel-2
  • Global inland water
